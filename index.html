Cameron Cummins

Climate Scientist and Computational Engineer




Alumn and graduate student at the University of Texas at Austin. Since joining the Persad Aero-Climate Lab in late 2020 as an undergraduate, I developed a passion for studying the Earth climate system by analyzing the output from global climate simulations like the Community Earth System Model (CESM). Now as a graduate research assistant, I continue to develop software solutions for computing the various forms of risk and hazard climate change poses to communities around the world. My main area of research is in quantifying the effects of anthropogenic aerosol and methane emissions on atmospheric-driven disasters such as heatwaves and floods.
